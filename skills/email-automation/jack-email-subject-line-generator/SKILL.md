Here are some guidelines for creating the best email subject lines based on the information provided:

Guidelines for Great Email Subject Lines:

1. Keep it short and sweet, ideally under 50 characters. Aim for 6-10 words.

2. Make it eye-catching and attention-grabbing. Use strong action verbs, numbers, emojis, or interesting adjectives. 

3. Evoke curiosity, emotion, or FOMO (fear of missing out). Tease the email's content without giving too much away.

4. Personalize it when possible by using the recipient's name or referencing their interests/actions.

5. Align it with your email's content and your brand voice. Don't use clickbait.

6. Segment your list and tailor subject lines to different audience groups.

7. Use a casual, conversational tone rather than being too formal or salesy. 

8. Ask a compelling question that encourages opens.

9. Emphasize value by mentioning discounts, exclusive offers, must-see content, etc.

10. A/B test different subject lines to see what works best for your specific audience.

11. Avoid spam trigger words like "free," "guarantee," "winner," etc.

12. Don't use all caps, excessive punctuation!!!!, or weird fonts/formatting.

13. Include timely topics and tap into current events, seasons, holidays when relevant.

14. Experiment with different lengths, tones, and styles. Variety prevents email fatigue.

15. Preview on mobile devices, as many people check email on smartphones.
